About Me

I am a highly motivated and detail-oriented BSc Biochemistry graduate with a strong foundation in molecular biology and bioinformatics. My research focuses on applying data-driven computational approaches to address critical biomedical challenges, particularly in the areas of antimicrobial resistance, molecular oncology, and genomic epidemiology.

My work integrates laboratory expertise with advanced bioinformatics to study pathogen genomics, resistance mechanisms, and microbial evolution. I have developed specialized computational tools like StaphScope and EcoliTyper that streamline genomic analysis and provide accessible solutions for bacterial genotyping and surveillance. These tools address the bioinformatics barrier in genomic epidemiology by offering user-friendly, efficient workflows for comprehensive pathogen characterization.

My research interests span genomic epidemiology of pathogens, antimicrobial resistance mechanisms, viral genomics, molecular oncology and biomarker discovery, plant and soil microbiomes, systems biology and multi-omics integration, and bioinformatics applications in public health. I am particularly passionate about developing computational solutions that bridge the gap between genomic data and actionable insights for clinical and public health applications.

Through my work as a bioinformatics analyst and research collaborator, I've provided end-to-end genomic analysis for graduate researchers, translating raw sequencing data into biologically meaningful insights. I also mentor undergraduate students, guiding them through in silico bioinformatics studies and fostering the next generation of computational biologists.

Publications & Projects

StaphScope: A species-optimized computational pipeline for rapid and accessible Staphylococcus aureus genotyping and surveillance
Beckley Brown
2025 | GitHub: https://github.com/bbeckley-hub/staphscope-typing-tool

Designed and developed a specialized, automated bioinformatics pipeline that integrates six essential S. aureus genotyping methods (MLST, spa typing, SCCmec analysis, antimicrobial resistance profiling, virulence factor screening, and plasmid detection) into a single, efficient workflow. StaphScope directly addresses the bioinformatics barrier in genomic surveillance by providing a user-friendly, locally executable tool that generates comprehensive, publication-ready reports.

Technical Implementation: Engineered in Python with a modular architecture for parallel execution, dynamically optimizing resource usage (CPU/RAM) via the psutil library. Distributed as a single-install Conda package to eliminate dependency conflicts and ensure reproducibility.

Validation & Impact: Achieved 100% concordance with established profiles of international reference strains (N315, USA300, MRSA252). Successfully characterized 17 clinical isolates, identifying key resistance mechanisms (e.g., mecA in 94% of MRSA) and virulence factors (e.g., PVL toxin). Benchmarked to be 1.7-4.3x faster than generalist platforms (Bactopia, Nullarbor).

Manuscript under review at BMC Genomics
